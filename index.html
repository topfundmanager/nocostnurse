Frequently Asked Questions

Find answers to common questions about our in-home nursing services and referral program.

In-home nursing care is typically available for children under 21 who have qualifying medical conditions such as congenital disorders, neurological conditions, respiratory issues, immune deficiencies, feeding disorders, or cardiovascular conditions. The individual must have Medicaid coverage and reside in Georgia.

Yes, at this time our in-home nursing services are focused exclusively on families residing in Georgia. This allows us to provide dedicated support and build strong relationships with local healthcare networks and Medicaid programs.

Medicaid is the primary insurance we work with for in-home nursing services. If you're unsure about Medicaid eligibility, please complete the questionnaire and our team can help guide you through the process or connect you with resources for enrollment.

After you submit the questionnaire, our team typically reviews submissions and reaches out within 1-2 business days. The full assessment process may vary depending on the complexity of each case and the responsiveness of the referred individual.

Referral bonuses range from $500 to $2,000 and are determined by several factors including the level of care required, the complexity of the case, and the services ultimately provided. Once a referral is accepted into our program, we'll communicate the specific payout amount.

Absolutely. We take privacy seriously and handle all submitted information with strict confidentiality. Your data is used solely for the purpose of processing referrals and providing care services. We never sell or share personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

If you're uncertain about eligibility, we encourage you to complete the questionnaire anyway. Our team will review the information and determine qualification. There's no penalty for submitting a referral that doesn't ultimately qualify—we appreciate every effort to connect families with care.
